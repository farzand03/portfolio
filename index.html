About Me

Hello, I'm Farzan, a third-year electrical engineering student with a passion for crafting websites and web applications from the ground up using various programming languages. Self-taught in this domain, I've embarked on a journey of continuous learning and exploration, honing my skills in web development with each project undertaken.

Alongside my university studies, I've had the privilege of working as an intern engineer at BHP, where I've gained firsthand experience in applying theoretical knowledge to real-world projects. This experience has provided me with invaluable insights into the practical aspects of electrical engineering, allowing me to enhance my problem-solving abilities and technical proficiency.

Beyond my academic and professional pursuits, I find immense fulfillment in giving back to my community through volunteering. Currently, I devote my time to Save the Children, contributing to their noble cause with dedication and compassion. Additionally, I serve as a STEM study buddy, sharing my knowledge and enthusiasm for science, technology, engineering, and mathematics with students eager to explore these fields.

In essence, I am a driven individual fueled by curiosity, creativity, and a desire to make a positive impact in both my academic journey and the wider community.

Electrical Engineering

Transitioning into university, as part of my degree, I was required me to undertake units involving python, c and matlab. In both python and c, I created simple games and simulations. These projects helped build my knowledge of fundamental concepts such as arrays, conditonal logic and statements, loops, file structures and much more.

For matlab, I have been learning to use the software to plot and visualise electromagnetic vector fields, for designing signal processing filters, and for solving and modelling mathematics problems.

Aside from the programming side of things, I have also been immersed into a world of complex mathematics, been exposed to configuring microcontrollers through using fundamental principles of boolean logic, and overall become a much better problem solver through my journey as an electrical engineering student.
